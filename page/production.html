Conception

Heroes began development in 2006 when Tim Kring, the creator of NBC's Crossing Jordan, conceived the idea of a large ensemble saga that would resonate with the audience. Feeling that traditional cop or medical dramas lacked characters big enough to save the world, Kring envisioned ordinary people discovering extraordinary abilities in a character-driven series rooted in reality. Casting directors Jason La Padura and Natalie Hart assembled a cast of new faces, including Milo Ventimiglia, who described the TV pilot as a character drama about everyday people with a heightened reality. Production designer Ruth Ammon played a crucial role in bringing this heightened reality to life.


Kring consulted with Damon Lindelof, the executive producer of Lost for advice on pitching the series to the network and navigating serialized drama. NBC's enthusiastic response led to the greenlight of the project. Kring, drawing on his six-year partnership with NBC as the showrunner for Crossing Jordan, pitched the pilot with intricate details, including a cliffhanger ending. The unaired pilot was initially reported to remain unreleased but was later included in the season one DVD set after a special screening at the 2006 San Diego Comic-Con International.

Writing

The writing process for Heroes involved assigning individual writers to develop scenes for specific characters, which were then combined to form complete episodes. This collaborative approach allowed all writers to contribute to each episode, facilitating quicker script completion and providing the filming crew with more flexibility in shooting at various locations. Tim Kring emphasized the importance of collaboration, especially in serialized dramas where understanding each character's development trajectory is crucial for planning multiple scenes at a single location.


Season two of Heroes faced challenges due to the Guild of America (WGA) strike, resulting in only 11 out of the planned 24 episodes being produced. This led to a restructuring of the season, incorporating only the Generations volume out of the originally planned three volumes. The subsequent volumes were reorganized, with Villains becoming the third volume in season three. Facing criticism for the complexity of the plot and numerous characters in the second and third volumes, the series aimed to return to its roots with the fourth volume, Fugitives. Creative differences and budget concerns led to the removal of producers Jesse Alexander and Jeph Loeb, prompting a refocusing on character development and simpler storytelling. The series also experienced changes in its writing staff, with Bryan Fuller joining and later leaving, while Greg Beeman was fired as director/producer by NBC in April 2009.

Music

Heroes: Original Soundtrack is the official Heroes soundtrack. It was released on March 18, 2008, by The NBC Universal Television, DVD, Music & Consumer Products Group. It contains recordings from the series' score by Wendy Melvoin and Lisa Coleman, including the show's theme music. The record also includes contributions from Panic! at the Disco, Wilco, Imogen Heap, Bob Dylan, Nada Surf, and David Bowie, among other artists and bands. One of the contributions is All Things Must Pass, the first new song in 10 years from The Jesus and Mary Chain. They released a re-recorded version of the song, titled All Things Pass, on their 2017 album Damage and Joy.


On February 29, 2008, NBC Universal Television, DVD, Music & Consumer Products Group released five music videos created by Heroes producer/director Allan Arkush, each combining show footage with songs from the soundtrack. The music videos were released on Zune and MSN.

Filming and visual effects

Eric Grenaudier and John Han of Stargate Digital are the visual effects supervisors, and Mark Spatny is the visual effects producer for Heroes. They work with visual effects animators Anthony Ocampo and Ryan Wieber, who are also from Stargate. The series' visual effects consist of blue screens and 2D and 3D animation. The visual effects for the series are created after the scene is shot in post-production, unlike special effects, which are incorporated into the scenes during filming. Notable visual effects include radioactive Ted in Company Man, Nathan flying from Mr. Bennet and The Haitian in Hiros, and Hiro's time-freezing encounter with the schoolgirl in the red bow in One Giant Leap. The special effects were coordinated by Gary D'Amico. Stunts were coordinated by Ian Ouinn, who also used blue screens to accomplish some of the stunts in the series, such as Nathan Petrelli flying away from Heidi during the car accident scene in Six Months Ago. Principal filming for Heroes occurred in Los Angeles and Santa Clarita, California. The Stargate Digital team was responsible for making Los Angeles look like international locations, such as India and Ukraine, using blue screens and set designs by Ruth Ammon, production designer for the series. The series was recorded using a single-camera setup.


In keeping with the comic book thematic elements used on Heroes, professional comic book artists Tim Sale and Alex Maleev were brought in to provide the artwork used within the series under the direction of Ruth Ammon, including the artwork of Isaac Mendez, and Mendez's metafictional comic book series, 9th Wonders!. Additionally, the font used in captions and credits for the show is reminiscent of traditional hand-rendered comic book lettering. It was created by Sale and is based on his handwriting style. Heroes was designed by production designer Ruth Ammon. Ammon was nominated for her work on Heroes twice by the Art Directors Guild and nominated for two Emmy Awards for excellence in art direction.
